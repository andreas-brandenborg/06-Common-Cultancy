Russia Invades Ukraine

Thursday, February 24th 2022, Ukraine is invaded by Russia.
The media is flooded with posts, prayers, and fundraisers - the World wants to help!
Years go by and the war continues; unfortunately, media coverage does not. While Ukraine fights on against the largest country on Earth, social media moves on.

The purpose of this website is to highlight the development of social media interactions on posts concerning the war in Ukraine.


Created by Andreas Brandenborg, Magnus Giemsa, Laurits Munk, and Victor Lotz.

Timeline of the Invasion

The following is a timeline of the war and the development of total interactions on facebook.

2022

February: Russia launches a full-scale invasion of Ukraine.

March: Heavy fighting continues in various regions, including Kyiv and Kharkiv.

April: Russia captures the southern city of Mariupol after a prolonged siege.

August: Ukrainian forces launch a counteroffensive, recapturing significant territories in the Kharkiv region.

November: The conflict enters a stalemate with both sides holding their positions.

2023

June: Ukraine launches a major counteroffensive, making gains in the southern regions.

August: Continued Ukrainian advances, but the conflict remains intense.

November: Winter campaigns begin, with both sides focusing on fortifying positions.

2024

March: Increased use of drones and missiles due to shortages of artillery ammunition.

April: Ukrainian forces make further gains in the spring campaigns.

August: Russian forces launch summer-autumn offensives, but Ukrainian defenses hold strong.

December: The war continues with no resolution in sight, and both sides suffer heavy casualties.

People are not against Ukraine but...

Since 2022, there has been 12.338 post against Ukraine. This is a lesser ammount compared to post who are neutral or pro-Ukraine. This means that the generel people who post on Facebook do not show affinity towards Russia. The problem, however, is the difference in attention.

From Social Media to Economic Support

While social media posts obviously don't directly help Ukraine, we will argue that voicing an issue will result in action from your government. Social media posts help shaping the public opinion and pressures governments to act. Highlighting the war's human cost and Ukraine's resilience amplify global awareness and solidarity, creating an environment where citizens demand action from their leaders.

This grassroots mobilization often translates into political advocacy, prompting governments to allocate resources, impose sanctions on aggressors, or increase aid to Ukraine. Furthermore, viral social media campaigns can inspire coordinated efforts among nations and international organizations, reinforcing the narrative that collective action is necessary. While social media posts themselves are not economic contributions, they play a critical role in mobilizing the political will that ultimately results in tangible financial and material support.
